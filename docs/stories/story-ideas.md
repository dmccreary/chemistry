- Marie Curie: Fire in the Glow – From clandestine “Flying University” classes in
    occupied Poland to exhaustion-filled nights refining pitchblende, show her stubborn
    pursuit of radioactivity despite poverty, sexism, and radiation’s unseen toll.

- Dmitri Mendeleev: The Table Dreamer – Follow the young Siberian exile who nearly
missed his exam when a snowstorm hit, then flash-forward to the famed dream sequence
that helped him see periodic patterns despite academic skeptics.

- Rosalind Franklin: Shadows and Light – Depict her battling smoky basement labs,
institutional sexism, and workplace rivalry while producing the Photo 51 diffraction
image that illuminated DNA’s structure.

- George Washington Carver: Roots of Renewal – Trace his childhood escape from
enslavement through nights of sketching plants under lamplight, leading to crop-
rotation chemistry that saved Southern soils.

- Ahmed Zewail: Femtosecond Vision – Portray the Egyptian student navigating culture
shock in the U.S., improvising equipment when funds were thin, ultimately inventing
femtochemistry and winning a Nobel.

- Alice Ball: The Forgotten Cure – Show the Hawaiian chemist isolating chaulmoogra oil
as the first effective leprosy treatment, all while fighting health issues and the
theft of her work after her early death.

- Tu Youyou: Artemisinin Quest – A “Project 523” storyline where she consults ancient
medical scrolls, tests extracts on herself, and perseveres through Cultural Revolution
hardships to defeat malaria.

- Chien-Shiung Wu: The Reversal – Chronicle her escape from war-torn China, life in
segregated dorms, and the all-night experiments overturning parity conservation—even
as the Nobel went elsewhere.

- Luis Miramontes: Pill Pioneer – Follow the young Mexican chemist who co-invented the
oral contraceptive while juggling family obligations, political pressures, and crude
lab equipment.

- Stephanie Kwolek: Kevlar Threads – A story about the daughter of Polish immigrants
confronting colleagues who doubted her polymer work—until the day she pulls a golden
fiber stronger than steel from a cloudy solution.
